# INTRODUCTION
UrbanHaul partners with a distributed network of riders to deliver food, parcels, groceries, and medical supplies across high-density cities. As the platform scales, leadership needs clearer visibility into rider performance, operational efficiency, delivery quality, and cost drivers across regions and delivery categories. Without a centralized analytics view, it is difficult to identify top-performing riders, detect burnout risk, optimize routing efficiency, and balance service quality with operational costs.

___

# BUSINESS PROBLEM

UrbanHaul operates a fast‑growing logistics platform with riders delivering food, parcels, groceries, and medical supplies across multiple urban markets. Despite rapid expansion, leadership faces a critical challenge: limited visibility into operational efficiency, financial performance, and rider productivity.

**Key Issues**:

- Fragmented Operational Insights
Leadership lacks a holistic view of performance across regions and delivery categories. Decisions on resource allocation, cost control, and service quality are reactive rather than strategic.
- Unclear Profitability Drivers
It remains difficult to identify which regions or delivery categories are most profitable, whether operational costs align with efficiency, and if service quality is improving over time.
- Limited Rider Performance Tracking
Rider productivity is not systematically measured across shifts, routes, or vehicle types. As a result, leadership cannot easily identify top performers, track trends, or monitor satisfaction.
- Hidden Operational Risks
Without visibility into workload distribution and rider engagement, risks such as burnout, low morale, or uneven scheduling remain undetected. Coaching, incentives, and route optimization are reactive instead of data‑driven.

__Impact__:

This lack of structured, data‑driven insights prevents UrbanHaul from:
- Optimizing resource allocation across regions and categories
- Improving rider engagement and retention
- Enhancing customer satisfaction through consistent service quality
- Driving sustainable growth through informed strategic decisions

___

# BUSINESS QUESTIONS

__Shift Performance__:
- How does rider performance vary across different shifts (morning, afternoon, evening, late‑night)?
  
**Vehicle Utilization**:
- How effectively do riders perform when using different vehicle types (bike, scooter, car, etc.)?
  
__Customer Ratings Over Time__:
- What are the trends in rider ratings over time, and are they improving or declining?
  
**Top and At‑Risk Performers**:
- Which riders consistently achieve high ratings, and which may require coaching or support to improve?

**Regional Efficiency & Profitability**:
- Which regions or cities deliver the highest efficiency and earnings per distance traveled?

__Delivery Categories & Revenue Drivers__:
- How does delivery volume vary by category and priority, and which categories generate the most revenue?

__Service Quality Trends__:
- Are customer ratings trending upward or downward across the platform?

# INSIGHTS

**Operational Overview**:
- Revenue & Efficiency: Fragile item deliveries in New York (morning shift, motorcycle) generated $350K revenue across 17K deliveries, with an average earning of $1.40 per km.
- Customer Ratings: Ratings fluctuated throughout the year, peaking mid‑year (6.0 in Aug–Sep) but dipping in Oct (5.4), suggesting seasonal or workload‑related service quality challenges.
- Delivery Priorities: A majority of deliveries were low priority (61%), yet high‑priority deliveries (37%) contributed significantly to revenue, highlighting the importance of balancing urgency with efficiency.
- Regional Efficiency: States like California, Texas, and New York showed strong operational efficiency, indicating potential focus areas for scaling.
- Rider Distribution: Top performers (e.g., Abigail Nelson with a perfect 10.0 rating) contrast sharply with poorly rated riders (e.g., Tyler Garcia at 2.9), revealing uneven service quality across the workforce.

__Rider Case Study: Aaron Lopez__
- Shift Performance: Aaron’s ratings across shifts (6.0–6.3) are consistent but not exceptional, showing reliability without standout performance.
- Vehicle Utilization: His earnings per km with a car (1.7) are competitive but similar to other vehicle types, suggesting no clear advantage in his current assignment.
- Customer Ratings: Overall rating of 6.2 is slightly above average but below top performers, indicating room for improvement in customer satisfaction.
- Burnout Risk: Dashboard flags Aaron as low risk, meaning his workload is balanced — but his middling ratings suggest engagement or service quality could be improved.

# RECOMMENDATION
 __Operational Level__:
- Prioritize High‑Revenue Deliveries: Allocate more resources to high‑priority categories since they drive significant revenue.
- Address Service Quality Variability: Implement training and incentive programs to reduce dips in customer ratings and improve consistency.
- Scale Efficient Regions: Focus growth strategies on states with strong operational efficiency while investigating weaker regions for improvement opportunities.
  
**Rider Level** (Illustrated by Aaron Lopez) :
- Customer Interaction Coaching: Provide Aaron with targeted coaching to raise his customer ratings closer to top performers.
- Vehicle Assignment Optimization: Test assigning Aaron to shorter‑distance deliveries with bikes or motorcycles to see if efficiency improves compared to car usage.
- Engagement & Incentives: Since burnout risk is low, Aaron could be incentivized to take on more challenging routes, balancing workload while boosting ratings.
